PARAMETER A - SCHOOL/CAMPUS SITE

SYSTEM - INPUTS & PROCESSES

  • S.1 The school campus/site meets the requirements on location
    1. Campus Master Site Development Plan
    2. Campus Master Site Development Plan (As built plan)
    3. Ayuti Site (upper main campus)

IMPLEMENTATION

  • I.1 Current policies on environmental management and sustainable development approaches are evident and cascaded into the faculty students and stakeholders
    1. Policies on environmental management and sustainable developments
      (trash segregation, carbon footprint reduction ('machine-free Fridays'), clean-up drive,
      austerity measures, energy efficiency and conservation program, aircon usage schedule)
    2. Environmental management plan of SLSU
    3. Environmental compliance (pollution control officer)
    4. Memo on relaxation of dress code due to environmental changes
  • I.2 The existing school site complies with the national standard requirements set by the government
    1. Compliance certificates
    2. Copies of TCT lease and proof of ownership
    3. SLSU Main Campus CTC Registration
    4. SLSU additional land titles registration
  • I.3 The institution conducts periodic analysis of water quality and disposes its wastes properly
    1. Water potability, waste management, pest control, MRF, and etc.

OUTCOMES

  • O.1 Campus-related accidents are minimal
    1. Certification from University Health Services
    2. Certificate from University Health Services
  • O.2 Campus is adaptable to changes in climate and resilient to tremors and storms
    1. QS Stars, Green UI Metrics, resiliency practices, evacuation & emergency plans
  • O.3 Campus environment and landscape are impressive and well-maintained
    1. Campus environment & landscape are well-maintained (pictures)
  • O.4 Permanence of the academic campus and buildings in the present site is ensured
    1. Building insurances
  • O.5 Number of guest lecturers/visitors/students visiting the campus or doing academic work has improved/increased
    1. Visitors and guest lecturers related data
  • O.6 The campus usually gets the praise of everyone who visits it
    1. Visitor's satisfaction survey results

BEST PRACTICES

  • B.1 Pedestrian-first campus master planning through a walkable academic town concept that integrates academic, cultural, and recreational nodes around a clear civic heart.
    1. Evidence 01
  • B.2 Climate- and resilience-responsive site design through tree-lined walks, shaded forecourts, green pockets, and open evacuation quadrangles
    1. Evidence 02
  • B.3 Phased campus expansion strategy backed by newly acquired land assets for annex development, ensuring long-term permanence and controlled growth rather than overcrowded short-term
    1. Evidence 03
